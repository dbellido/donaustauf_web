Donaustauf is located 10 km East of Regensburg, city which continues the legacy of Radasbona Celtic settlement and the Roman camp of Castra Regina. There, in the location where the Bavarian forest starts, a hill is raised about 100 m above the Danube. A 230 m * 150 m plateau stands at the top of the hill and is protected with drastic height differences. Due to such topographic conditions as well as their privilege position for the Danube waterway domination, the hill has been predestined for a grand Settlement since ancient times.

Celtic period

Archaeology excavations performed since 1960 in the area near the cemetery have brought to light evidences that confirm the use of the hill as a Mountain Manor and evidences of activity in the Late Hallstatt and Early La Téne periods. New excavations in 1981-1982 discovered new evidences such as a huge wall more than 6 meters wide. The wall was built with large stone blocks in the exterior part while the interior was formed by loosely stones. Furthermore, in front of this construction, massive earth movements in the North Slope of the castle were performed. Therefore, this a possible indicator for the presence of a well-organized community. Additionally, these presupposes are supplemented by extensive archaeological material, including high quality ceramics, or an iron fibula with enamel and coral deposits. These findings have been dated around 500 before Christ.

Other findings from the Iron Age are preserved in the valley surrounding the castle. Its support the idea than the today preserved east and south walls sit on the prehistoric structures. The terrace structure of the castle suggests to researchers the idea that the southern tip of the mountain in Celtic times, although no complete evidences are founded. The size of the plant, and the topographically prominent position in visual contact with the Danube are the evidences used by the experts to ensure the idea of the Celtic structure. Other fortifications in the area support this idea, such as the castle hill in Kallmünz. As elsewhere in the Celtic excavations of the area, the hillfort of Donaustauf was abandoned in 350 before Christ.

Early medieval Castellum

In the Middle Ages the Donaustauf Castle became the scene of numerous battles and conflicts. A route through the history of the castle allow to understand the turbulent history of the area in this period as well as to comprise the importance of the castle where just ruins are today visible.

During the Carolingian period, the castle hill was a part of the vast royal Forest of Sulzbach. In 1911, Conrad I was elected king of the East Francia by the rulers of Saxonia, Swabia and Bavaria. Conrad I, who was already duke of Franconia and son of the Duke of Thuringia, found very difficult to estabish his autority over the growing power of the dukes. Indeed, Conrad I died due to the injueries caused by one of the battles with Arnulf, Duke of Bavaria. Within this background of continuous clashes between the Bavarian duke and the King, Conrad I gave in 1914 the Imperial forest to the monastery of St. Emmeram, whhose abbot also exercised the episcopate at this time. Under this donation, the King aimed to reinforce the Regensburg as an institution in his favour.

Being Tuto bishop of Regensburg (abbot ex oficio), the first of reference to the castle is found. This document dated between 914 and 930, mentiones arrangement in the castle area Castellum quod dicitur Stufo. This first structure was mounted over the remains of the Celtics structures and in the same dimensions. The excavations in 982 rpoved that wood and earth walls were erected over the celtic structures. Probably, this first castle was used as refuge at that time. Afther that the castle was seriously improved, presumably in order to be protected against the Hungarian raids, which during the first half of the 10th century affected the Upper Palatinate. This first period of invasions was finished in 1955 when some military leaders are hanged in Regensburg, after the victory of Otto I the great in the battle of Lechfeld.

He was one of the first German bishops to do this, and his example in this was much copied across Germany in the years following. He continued his father's work of unifying all German tribes into a single kingdom and greatly expanded the king's powers at the expense of the aristocracy. Through strategic marriages and personal appointments, Otto installed members of his family in the kingdom's most important duchies. Otto transformed the Roman Catholic Church in Germany to strengthen the royal office and subjected its clergy to his personal control. ITN-DCH project aims to analyze, design, research, develop and validate the innovative muti-disciplinary and intersectorial research training framework that covers the whole lifecycle of digital CH research for a cost-effective preservation, documentation, protection and presentation. Donaustauf castle is one of the case studies where the different fellow are applying their research. With the support of ArcTron3D, the Marie Curie fellows are being trained in laser scanner and light structured methods in Cultural Heritage for high resolutions 3D surveys.



Activities

Photogrammetry

Photogrammetry is the science of creating 3D information from photographs. Nowadays, just a commercial camera is required to acquire the images. From terrestrial or aerial plattformas photogrammetry is one of the most suitable techniques in the digiization of Cultural Heritage.

Laser scanner

The purpose of a 3D laser scanner is to create a point cloud of the surface of a objetc. Nowadays there a wide amount of scanners in the market and they can acquire information in small and large scenes. The three principles of laser scanners include Time of flight (TOF), Phase Shift and Triangulation-based systems.

Read more

Structured light scanner

A structured light scanner is a 3D intruments to achieve the 3D shape of a object. It uses projection light patterns and a camera system (usually two cameras). Projection band of lights onto an object, produces line distorted from the different perspectives, and it is used to extract the 3D information about the object.
